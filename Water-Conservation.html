Water Conservation at Campus of KSRDPR University, Gadag

Introduction:

Karnataka State Rural Development University, new site is spread in Mallasamudra, Kalsapura and Nagavi Village, 7 to 8 kilometers away from Gadag city. University has undulating landscape which is includes with Hill, Plateau and plain places. East side of university site covered by Hill and west side having a gentle slope.

Water conservation:

University site area comes in tropical zone, monsoon rains are the main water source for University activities like DeSSi Project, Nursery Project and Construction of Buildings.



Field and Geospatial software Analysis:

Based on the field survey and analysis of data using geospatial software 6 suitable locations to construct water conservation pits.

Water Conservation Pits:

In the entire area, 6 Water Conservation Pits are proposed with having a size of 3m * 3m * 3m, which are in the lowest elevation of the entire area and nearby main stream for harvesting the excess runoff water.

Water capacity and use:

The one pit will store about approximately 27000 liters, and the 6 pits will store approximately 1,62,000 liters at a time. The stored water can be utilized for construction of various buildings , DeSSi Project, Nursery project, and Smruti Vana though the drip and sprinkler system for protective irrigation.

Results:

1. The conserved water can be utilized for ongoing construction activities
2. The water stored can improve the groundwater level in the surrounding area
3. Water can be transported to DeSSi Project, Smruti Vana, Sbarmati Asharam Gardan, Nursery Project and Live stock as per the requirement.
